Calculating fairness statistics¶

I decided to look at the 'credit scoring' dataset. The protected variable, according to which I am calculating fairness statistcs is age - more precisely, whether someone is very young (under 30). I am using two tree-based models - XGBoost and Random Forest Classifier. It seems that the models might be biased against younger people.

Task 1¶

  • Demographic parity: $\frac{0.5}{0.65}\approx 0.77$ - which is almost in the 'fair' brackets.
  • Equal opportunity: $\frac{0.5}{0.75}\approx 0.67$
  • Predictive parity: $\frac{0.5}{0.25} = 2$

Task 2¶

Subtask 2¶

In the plot, we can observe that according to such coefficients as equal opportunity and predictive parity, the model doesn't discriminate against young people. The statistical parity ratio shows us that the model might be biased against young people - it predicts credit problems for them more often than for the older population.

Subtask 3¶

I trained Random Forest Classifier. When looking at this plot and the one from the previous task it seems a little better - statistical parity ratio is a bit smaller and so is the equal opportunity ratio. It doesn't really differ much.

Subtask 4¶

I removed the age variable and applied the resampling technique. While just removing the age doesn't help much with the statistical parity ratio (maybe because the informatio is still contained in for example the number of dependants?), applying resampling seems to change a lot.

Subtask 5¶

All three models (XGBoost, Random Forest Classifier, XGBoost + resampling) have accuracy of around 0.94, which is pretty high. Therefore there isn't much correlation between fairness and model performance. Which might suggest, that the model might have been to some extent discriminating against young people.

Dataset¶

This dataset contains the data about the scores of students of two different school in Portugal in math and Portuguese. The goal is to predict the final score of a student, using information about their lifestyle and background. I am going to focus only on predicting G3 - according to the description of the problem G1 and G2 are highly correlated with G3.

def plot_roc_curve(fpr, tpr, label=None):
    plt.figure(figsize=(12,10))
    plt.plot(fpr, tpr, linewidth=2, label=label)
    plt.plot([0,1],[0,1], "k--")
    plt.axis([0,1,0,1])
    plt.xlabel("False Positive Rate")
    plt.ylabel("True Positive rate")

def pf_xgboost_classifier_categorical(model, df):
    df.loc[:, df.dtypes == 'object'] =\
        df.select_dtypes(['object'])\
        .apply(lambda x: x.astype('category'))
    return model.predict_proba(df)[:, 1]
