Permutation-based Variable Importance¶

Here we can see permutation importance for a random forest model. What's interesting - the most important feature is hypertension, while previously it usually was the age. Also, the importance of those top features seems a little low.

What's even more interesting - for most of the features importance is negative, and for some of them, the negative importance is a lot bigger than positive importance of even the most important features. That means, we actually get better results if we shuffle those features.

Comparing different models¶

We can see, that for 3 other models we actually get more predictive feature importances. For all 3 models (LR, AB and SVM) age is by far, the most important feature. Every model has different next-most-important features. Also distributions are different, while in SVM age is 6 times more important than the next most important feature (glucose level), and next features are even less important, AdaBoost has 2 dominant features: age and work_type_children (which are strongly correlated). When analyzing the logistic regression it seems that work type and smoking is also pretty important (not as much as the age though).

When it comes to negative importance features, SVM has only a few features with very weak negative importance. AdaBoost has one feature with strong negative importance - BMI (same as Random Forest), and Logistic Regression haev few moderately strong negative importance features, one of which being bmi, as we've seen in previous models.
